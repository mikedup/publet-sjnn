SOCIAL JUSTICE NEWS NEXUS

EACH OF US IS A ROLLING WHEEL. TOGETHER WE ARE A DRIVING FORCE. WE ARE GOING TO TELL YOU MORE.









The Social Justice News Nexus brings together reporters, community watchdogs, and Medill journalism students to cover Chicago’s pressing issues across a variety of media platforms. 




The project, supported by the Robert R. McCormick Foundation and housed at Northwestern University, launched in January 2014 with an inaugural group of 14 community fellows and eight graduate student fellows. The fellows are working on stories related to how drugs and the nation’s drug war impact the communities in and around Chicago. 




The first series of stories will be released in different media outlets and on the Social Justice News Nexus website in June 2014. The stories along with related community events are meant to explore drug policy and — in the future — other complicated topics through a social justice lens, contributing to the public debate and helping to inform public policy.
